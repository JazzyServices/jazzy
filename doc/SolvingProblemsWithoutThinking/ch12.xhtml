Puzzle 12

In Peg Solitaire, the starting position has 32 pegs and one vacant hole as in the diagram below. There is only one type of move that can be made which is for one peg to jump over a neighbouring peg into a vacant hole. The peg that was jumped over is then removed from the board. The task is to have one peg remaining; and that peg must be in the centre of the board.

Puzzle 12 step 1

To be able to solve Peg Solitaire through trial and error could take a very long time. So firstly, we try to solve a simpler puzzle.
Make the correct jumps so that the three pegs in the box are removed and the fourth peg returns to its original position.

Puzzle 12 step 2

First remove the three pegs in the small box then the six pegs in the larger box. The six pegs on the left should remain.

Puzzle 12 step 3

Remove the pegs from the L-shaped box. You should be left with the two pegs outside the box from which you should be able to finish with one peg in the centre.

Puzzle 12 step 4

First remove the boxes of three pegs, then the boxes of six pegs (clockwise from the bottom), then the L-shape. You should be left with two pegs from which you should be able to complete the puzzle.
