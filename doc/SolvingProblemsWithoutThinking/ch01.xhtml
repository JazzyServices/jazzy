Puzzle 1 part 2

An ant crawls along the edges of a box starting from point A and finishing at point B (see diagram below). There are six directions in which the ant can move as it travels from one corner to an adjacent corner. (Clearly, at any one corner, the ant only has a choice of three of these six directions.)
  • Up
  • Down
  • Left
  • Right
  • Away (from the observer)
  • Toward (the observer)
If the following restrictions apply, how can the ant get from A to B.
  • In the first move the ant must move upwards from A (it is not allowed to move right nor away.)
  • In the last move the ant must move upwards toward B (is not allowed to move right nor away.)
  • The ant is not allowed to visit the same corner twice.
A B
This is slightly more challenging; but if we simply cross out the four lines that ant isn't allowed to move along (two from A and two towards B), then we can see that it is the trivial puzzle we saw in part 1 (drawn in a slightly different way).

Lesson 1 – Eliminate the Impossible

The technique we used to solve Puzzle 1 (part 2) is one of the most crucial techniques in all puzzle-solving: eliminate the impossible. One we had done this, the solution became clear.
